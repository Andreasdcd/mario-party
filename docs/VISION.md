# Game Vision: Board Game Party

**Version:** 0.1 - Initial Vision
**Date:** 2026-04-20

## Elevator Pitch

*"Mario Party meets skill challenges - a turn-based party board game where players compete through strategy, quick thinking, and clever decision-making instead of button-mashing minigames."*

## Core Pillars

### 1. Turn-Based Board Movement
- Players move around a board in turns
- Each tile triggers different events
- Strategic positioning matters
- Predictable flow with unpredictable outcomes

### 2. Skill Challenges Over Minigames
- Challenges test **decision-making**, not reflexes
- Math, logic, strategy, risk assessment
- Feel like game mechanics, not homework
- Short (15-45 seconds), clear, impactful

### 3. Accessible Yet Strategic
- Easy to understand in 2 minutes
- Depth emerges through play
- Everyone can compete regardless of gaming skill
- Luck + skill balance

### 4. High Replayability
- System-driven, not content-heavy
- Procedural elements where appropriate
- Multiple viable strategies
- Every game feels different

### 5. Polished & Intentional
- Fake 3D / isometric aesthetic
- Clear UI and feedback
- Smooth animations and transitions
- Nothing feels placeholder

## What This Game IS

✅ A strategic party game for friends/family
✅ Competitive but approachable
✅ Short sessions (15-30 minutes)
✅ Offline, local multiplayer (hotseat)
✅ Replayable through smart systems
✅ Educational value as bonus, not requirement

## What This Game IS NOT

❌ A pure educational tool
❌ Real-time action game
❌ Online multiplayer focused
❌ Content-heavy (1000 minigames)
❌ A Mario Party clone

## Target Experience

**Player sits down for a game:**
1. Picks character and sees simple board
2. Rolls/chooses movement
3. Lands on tile → event triggers
4. Faces quick challenge or decision
5. Gains/loses resources or position
6. Next player's turn
7. First to goal (or highest score after X turns) wins

**Player feels:**
- Engaged, not bored
- In control, not purely lucky
- Competitive without frustration
- Smart when making good decisions
- Eager to play again

## Inspiration Sources

**Core Loop:** Mario Party (board movement, turn structure)
**Challenge Style:** Brain Age, Professor Layton (quick thinking)
**Tone:** Fall Guys, Overcooked (light, fun, competitive)
**Aesthetic:** Monument Valley, Captain Toad (isometric, colorful, clear)

## Example Session Flow

```
[Game Start]
→ 4 players on START tile
→ Player 1 rolls → lands on "Blue Tile" → gains 3 coins
→ Player 2 rolls → lands on "Challenge Tile" → faces math duel with Player 1
   - Challenge: "Pick two numbers that sum to 15 - closest wins!"
   - Player 2 picks (8, 7) = 15 → wins 5 coins from Player 1
→ Player 3 rolls → lands on "Event Tile" → triggers shop event
   - Spend 10 coins to steal a star? Yes/No
→ Player 4 rolls → lands on "Red Tile" → loses 2 coins
→ [Next round begins]
```

After 10 rounds, player with most stars + bonus coins wins.

## Success Criteria (Vertical Slice)

A successful v0.1 means:
- 2-4 players can complete a full game loop
- Game feels fun and competitive
- Challenges are quick, clear, and satisfying
- Board movement works smoothly
- UI is clean and understandable
- Someone who isn't us enjoys playing it

---

**Vision Summary:**
Build a small, excellent party board game that **feels like a game first**, integrates skill challenges naturally, and leaves players wanting to play again.
